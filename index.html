2. Receive a bounded next step

3. Notice a pattern

4. Prepare for therapy

Beacon drafts: “Avoidance increased on three high-pressure days. The breathing exercise helped twice.”

5. Stay in control

The patient edits, approves or deletes the summary before sharing it.

Clear boundaries

Questions you should ask

Is Beacon replacing my therapist?

No. Beacon supports reflection, exercises and preparation between appointments. Your therapist remains responsible for your care.

Will everything I enter be shared?

No. Nothing enters a therapist summary without you choosing to include it. You remain in control.

Does Beacon diagnose me from my mood or behaviour?

No. Beacon may show possible patterns, but it labels them as observations rather than diagnoses or clinical conclusions.

What happens if I need urgent help?

Beacon stops the normal experience and directs you toward immediate human and crisis-support options.

Why not use a general-purpose chatbot?

Beacon is designed around bounded support, patient control and continuity with qualified human care—not unrestricted conversation.
